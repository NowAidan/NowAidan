Atropa bella-donna

Atropa bella-donna (also known as deadly nightshade) is a toxic flower in the nightshade family. It is native to Europe and Western Asia. It has also been introduced into some parts of Canada.

Toxicity

If ingested the folliage and berries from the flower are extremely toxic, with even the slightest touch of this flower being harmful. The toxins present can also cause a sudden state of confusion and/or hallucinations. To make it worse, the effects of Atropa bella-donna are unpredictable.

History

Atropa bella-donna has an exstenive history of use as a medicine, cosmetic, and poison. It was first known by various folk names, the most popular being "deadly nightshade". The modern day name for it was bestowed by Carl Linna (1707-1778). Atropa was chosen due to the poisonous properties of the plant. And bella-donna (meaning "beautiful woman" in Italian) to represent the plant being used for cosmetic purposes.
